As an aspiring engineering student, I am fascinated by the world around us. More precisely, my fascination stems from the fundamental mathematical description of the physical world. This coupled with mechanical interpretation allows us to explore and explain very complex natural systems. My journey in becoming an engineer is to utilize those mathematical and physical descriptions and create useful innovation that can forward science and technology.

As a dual degree student working to complete four undergraduates degrees in Applied Physics, Mathematics, Electrical Engineering, and System Sciences and Engineering; I am interested in leveraging my knowledge from these different fields and apply them to understand non-linear dynamical systems through the lens of modern control theory. Working intimately with modeling of systems in hopes to introduce controls to gain further insights into the possible explanation of some natural system.

My academic experience in optimal control and adaptive control has been with autonomous drone vehicles, i.e. quadcopter and motor car. My research experience has been working with neurological systems viewed from the lens of modern control theory. My hope is to work in the field of controls engineering and optimization.
